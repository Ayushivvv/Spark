# Problem Space

We are investigating the emotional impact that first- and second-year university students experience when trying to develop themselves socially and build relationships through support circles and friendships. We want to understand the emotional aftermath of events such as “how may a student feel walking back to their room after a social event? Hope, relief, confusion, emptiness, regret?” as well as consider the emotional asymmetry within perceiving connections, when one student may find the connectivity meaningful, which the other does not. This leads to quiet emotional dissonance, and in many cases, incoming students may feel cumulative emotional fatigue as well from the repetitive cycle of “meeting people who you may not keep,” which may generate resignation, withdrawal, and disengagement for the student. The biggest aspect of entering university is developing relationships on large campuses and engaging in multiple events in a short period of time in a new and diverse environment. We also want to help foster these social connections among individuals at events hosted by groups, clubs, orientation leaders, or faculty. We want to observe, “how freshman university students may feel about the crowded events during the beginning of their semesters; intimidating, impersonal, or exhausting?” and “how do they embody discomfort? Whether by standing alone, scanning the room or rehearsing their introductions internally?” After initiating relations during those major commencement events, we want to help students maintain the flow and connection even after those events end.

# Stakeholders

**Primary:** 1st & 2nd Year Students  
**Secondary:** 3rd & 4th Year Students, TA/Professors; involved in organizing events for the incoming students (1st/2nd years) and model social stability and represent belongingness within the students.

# Why this problem matters

We found that a crucial objective of first/second year oriented activities held by the university integrates engaging within the full university life and developing a sense of belonging through meaningful social connections. These early years are often when students form their first impressions of whether they “fit” within the university environment. When connections are formed during this period, they tend to create familiarity, comfort, and confidence over time. Conversely, when students struggle to sustain early social interactions, the experience is often internalized as personal failure, leading to emotional withdrawal and a reduced willingness to engage. Reflecting on our own transition into the third year, we observed that many enduring friendships and familiar social circles had already been established within the first two years. This highlights how early social experiences can quietly shape the remainder of a student’s university journey. These social engagements shape more meaningful experiences that foster long-term academic and professional participation. So the initial aspect of developing their social selves and maintaining those relationships is where we want to help and make it easier to maintain.

# How this app helps

This app is designed to support students in navigating the emotional and social challenges of university life, especially during the critical early years. By providing features such as event scheduling, support circle management, and prompts for reflection and connection, the app helps students:

- **Track and maintain social connections** formed at events, making it easier to follow up and nurture relationships.
- **Reflect on emotional experiences** after social events, helping students process feelings and recognize patterns in their social interactions.
- **Reduce emotional fatigue** by offering tools to manage and organize social commitments, making the process less overwhelming.
- **Foster a sense of belonging** by connecting students with groups, clubs, and peers, and encouraging ongoing engagement beyond initial meetings.
- **Support event organizers** (upper-year students, TAs, professors) in facilitating more meaningful and inclusive social experiences for incoming students.

Ultimately, the app aims to make the process of building and sustaining social relationships more intentional, supportive, and emotionally healthy for all students.
